Subject, Predicate and object

A sentence is divided into two parts:
1. Subject
2. Predicate

1. Subject:
The name of a person or a thing in a sentence we are speaking about is called subject.
or The naming part of the sentence is called subject.

2. Predicate:
The part of the sentence which tells something about the subject is called predicate.
or The telling part of the sentence is called predicate.
or Without subject the rest part of the sentence is called predicate.

Example: He recites the Holy Quran.
Ahmad eats the meal.
Note: "He & Ahmad" are subjects and "rest parts of the sentences" are predicates.

Object:The reciever of the action of the verb is called object.
Example: In the above sectences "Holy Quran and meal" are objects.

There are three types of object

  1. Direct
  2. Indirect
  3. Object Complement

Pronoun Meaning:
Pro means for/in place of and noun means name.

(1)Pronoun Definition:
A word which is used in a sentence instead of a noun to avoid its repetition is called pronoun.
Example: Ahmad is good boy. He likes prayer.

(2)Persons in Pronoun
There are three persons in pronoun:
  1. 1st Person:
    2. Speaker
    eg: I, We.
  2. 2nd Person:
    3. Listener or person who we are talking to
    eg: You.
  3. 3rd Person:
    4.  Person who we are talking about
    eg: He, She, It, They.
    Note: Third personal pronouns are, strictly speaking, Demonstrative pronouns.
Kinds of Pronoun:
There are 11 kinds of pronoun
  1. Subjective/Nominative Pronoun:
    2.  Use as a subject. egI go to school.
  2. Objective/Accusative Pronoun:

  3. Possessive Pronoun:
    4.  Shows temporary ownership.
    follow verbs or at the end of the sentence.

  4. Indefinite Pronoun:
    5.  Use for unknown person, place or thing. Always follow singular verb.
    eg: anyperson, everybody, something, no one, one, all, none, few, many, more, others.
    Most of these words may also be used as adjective. eg:Any fool can do that. Some milk was split.

    Indefinite-Personal Pronouns Agreement:
    1. Personal pronouns are used according to the context.
    2. Today it is more usual to use a plural pronouns. eg: Each of them had thier share. Anybody can do it if they try.
  5. Reflexive Pronoun:
    6.  Pronoun which ends at self/selves and use after the verb.
    It shows the action turns back upon the subject.
    It act as a subject.
    eg:I told myself.
  6. Emphatic/Intensive Pronoun:
    7.  Pronoun which ends at self/selves and use before the verb.It creates emphasis.
    eg: I myself told. We saw the Prime Minister himself. I will do it myself.

    NOTE:
    Reflexive and Emphatic pronouns are collectively called Compound Personal Pronouns.
    Sometimes self is used as a noun. eg: He cares for nothing but self. He thinks much of self.
  7. Reciprocal Pronoun:
    8.  Shows mutual relationship.
    These are compound pronouns.
    eg: Each other- Use in spesking of tow persons.
    one another- Use in speaking of more than tow persons.
    NOTE:
    But these rules are no longer strictly observed.
    eg: We can say that "Three brothers quarreled with each other."

  9. Distributive Pronoun:
    10.  Talk about each and every person separately.
    orRefers to persons or things one at a time.
    eg:Each, either, neither, any, no one, none, everyone.

    Rules:
    1. These pronouns are always singular.
      2.
    2. These pronouns are followed by singular verb.
      3.
    3. Each:
      4.  Denote every one of a number of persons or things.
      Three positions:
      1. At the start of the sentence, eg:Each of the men recieved reward.Each of the men recieved 20 rupees.
      2. At the end of the sentence but after the numeral eg:These men recieved 20 rupees each
      3. Just after the main verb, eg:These men recieved each 20 rupees.
    4. Either, Neither
      5.  Use in speaking of tow persons or things.
      Either: means the one or the other of the tow.
      Neither: means not the one nor the other of the tow.
    5. Any, No one, None
      6. Use in speaking of more than tow persons or things
    6. Each, either, neither:
      7. Can be used as adjectives, they are followed by singular noun. eg:Each book is yours. Neither accusation is true. Either country is beautiful.
    7. Each/Every
      8.  Each: Indicates tow or more items, when we are thinking of the items individually.
      egEach student recieved a book.
      Every: Indicate three or more items, when we are thinking of the items collectively.
      eg:Every student needs to attend the meeting.

  10. Interrogative Pronoun:
    11.  Ask question, use at the start of the sentence, pronoun+verb.
    1. who: use for human, subjective case
    2. whose: possessive case
    3. whom: objective case
    4. when: use for time
    5. why: use for reason
    6. where: use for place
    7. what: use for general questions
    8. which: use for specific questions
    9. how: use for manner
  11. Relative Pronoun:
    12.  add more information, use at the middle of the sentences.
    1. who: Follow verb
    2. whom: follow pronoun
    3. whose: Follow noun
    4. which
    5. that
    Examples:
    Who are you?(I.P)
    The person who runs on the road.(R.P)
    NOTE: who, whom, whose use for human
    which for living things/animals and that for non-living things(These are interchangable too.)

NOTE: Interogative Adjective: whose, which, what + noun
Exclamatory Adjective: How, what use at the start of the sentence and verb use at the end of the sentence.

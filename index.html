Subject, Predicate and object

A sentence is divided into two parts:
1. Subject
2. Predicate

1. Subject:
The name of a person or a thing in a sentence we are speaking about is called subject.
or The naming part of the sentence is called subject.

2. Predicate:
The part of the sentence which tells something about the subject is called predicate.
or The telling part of the sentence is called predicate.
or Without subject the rest part of the sentence is called predicate.

Example: He recites the Holy Quran.
Ahmad eats the meal.
Note: "He & Ahmad" are subjects and "rest parts of the sentences" are predicates.

Object:The reciever of the action of the verb is called object.
Example: In the above sectences "Holy Quran and meal" are objects.

There are three types of object

  1. Direct
  2. Indirect
  3. Object Complement

Noun Cases:
There are 9 cases of noun:
  1. Subjective/Nominative case:
    eg: Ali send Ahmad massage.
  2. Objective case:
    eg: Ali send Ahmad massage.
  3. Dative case:
    eg: Ali send Ahmad massage.
  4. Possessive case:
    eg: Ahmad, Pakistan's national flag has two colors.
  5. Vocative case:
    eg: Ahmad, Pakistan's national flag has two colors.
  6. Object of Preposition:
    eg: Ahmad, my student went to home.
  7. Appositive case:
    eg: Ahmad, my student went to home.
  8. Accompaniment case:
    eg: Ahmad, Pakistan's national flag has two colors.
  9. Objective Complement case:
    eg: We elected Ahmad our leader.

Pronoun:

We will discuss three things in pronoun

  1. Definition
  2. Persons in Pronoun
  3. Kinds of Pronoun
(1)Pronoun Definition:
A word which is used in a sentence instead of noun to avoid its repeation is called pronoun.
Example: Ahmad is good boy. He likes prayer.

(2)Persons in Pronoun
There are three persons in pronoun:
  1. 1st Person:
    2.  eg: I, We.
  2. 2nd Person:
    3.  eg: You.
  3. 3rd Person:
    4.  eg: He, She, It, They.
Kinds of Pronoun:
There are 11 kinds of pronoun
  1. Subjective Pronoun:
    2.  Use as a subject. egI go to school.
  2. Objective Pronoun:
    3.  Use as an object. egI talk with Ahmad.I talk with him.
  3. Possessive Pronoun:
    4.  Shows ownership. egThis pen is yours.
    Person Subjective Pronoun Possessive Pronoun Possessive Adjective Objective Pronoun
    1st Person I Mine My Me
    We Ours Our Us
    2nd Person You Yours Your You
    3rd Person He His His Him
    She Hers Her Her
    It Its Its It
    They Thiers Thier Them
  4. Indefinite Pronoun:
    5.  Use for unknown person, place or thing.
    eg: anyperson, everybody, something, no one, one, all, many, more.
  5. Reflexive Pronoun:
    6.  Use self after the verb.
    eg:I told myself.
  6. Emphatic/Intensive Pronoun:
    7.  Use self before the verb.
    eg: I myself told.
  7. Reciprocal Pronoun:
    8.  Shows mutual relationship.
    eg: Each other- Tow Persons
    one another- More than tow persons
  8. Demonstrative Pronoun:
    9.  Points out something, person or place
    This- For near and Singular
    That- For far and Singular
    These- For near and plural
    Those- For far and plural
  9. Distributive Pronoun:
    10.  Talk about each and every person separately
    Each, either, everyone, neither.
  10. Interrogative Pronoun:
    11.  Ask question, use at the start of the sentence, pronoun+verb.
    1. who
    2. whose
    3. whom
    4. which
    5. when
    6. why
    7. where
    8. what
    9. how
  11. Relative Pronoun:
    12.  need more information, use at the middle of the sentences.
    1. who
    2. whom
    3. whose
    4. which
    5. that
    Examples:
    Who are you?(I.P)
    The person who you ran on the road.(R.P) NOTE: who, whom, whose use for human
    which for living things and that for non-living things

Exclamatory Adjective: How, what use at the start of the sentence and verb use at the end of the sentence.
